What is Productive Civil Discourse?

  • Willingness to consiider and listen
  • Constructive discussion to perpetuate ideas
  • Find common ground

Productive Civil Discourse

The purpose of civility is not to ensure that the conversation is polite or flows smoothly. It is to ensure that the conversation fosters democratic goals.

Discourse soley governed by politeness silences marginalized voices in an effort to minimize conversational friction.

Perceptions of Online Discourse

Compared to other places where people discuss politics, social media discussions are:

  • Less respectful
  • Less civil
  • Less focused on policy
  • Less informative
  • Less likely to come to resolution
  • Angrier

Strategies for Engaging in Productive Discourse

Get comfortable taking your won emotional temperature

  • When scrolling through your news feed, it is difficult to take account of your own emotional response

The best way to be heard is to unintuitively spend less time talking

  • Take this time to genuinly listen to the opponent and ask questions
  • Restate discussants view in your own words. Did I get that right?
  • It feels like you are giving up your ground, but research shoes opponent is more likely to move towards your opinion with this method

Can be helpful to remind both discussants of common ground (shared values)

Middle Ground vs. Common Ground

Middle Ground: if two parties attempt to meet in the middle, often end up ramping up own opinion in hopes to sway result their way

Common Ground: finding shared values in opinions of each party
