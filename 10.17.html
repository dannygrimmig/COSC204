What is Copyright?

The Problem with Software

Software is a nontangible, intellectual object.

Tangible objects are (or at least can be) nonexclusionary: they are able to be owned by more than one person at a time by way of lossless copies.

Because intellectual objects are abstract, the law only protects their physical manifestations (not the ideas themselves)

The Argument for Digital Copyright

Premise 1:: Stealing a tangible object is moreally wrong.

Premise 2:: Making an unauthorized copy of a proprietary software program is identical to stealing a tangible object..

Conclusion:: It is morally wrong to make an unauthorized copy of proprietary software.

Patents, Trademarks, and Trade Secrets

Other ways to protect Intellectual Property

Patents: 20 year monopoly on expression or implementation. Patented inventions must be useful, novel, and nonobvious

Trademarks: Protected use of a symbol, phrase, or name strongly aassociated with a product or service

Trade Secrets: Inforation that is crucial to the operation of some product

Key Takeaways

Copyright disputes are'nt new; but easy, lossless, costless copying is a new feature of the digital age

International Privacy laws grant the owner the right to control and produce physical manifestations or expression of the work

Making authorized digital copy has some important similarities and differences to/from stealing physical objects
