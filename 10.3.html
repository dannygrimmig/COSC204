Privacy & Security I

What is Privacy

Class Ideas:

  • Freedom from observation
  • Protect own info: from collection, usage, selling, access

Brief History of Privacy

In Western Thought: Athenian's drew a distinction between public life and domestic life

14th Century: people went to court for eavesdropping or opening/reading personal letters

United States: strength of the need for privacy might have roots in huge distances between homesteads

Historically: privacy has been linked to wealth. Poorer people live/lived in multifamily households, with less privacy.

Prosser's Privacy (1960): Four torts that would aallow someone whose privaaacy is violated to sue for damages.

  • Intrusion upon seclusion or solitude, or into private affairs
  • Public disclosure of embarrising private facts
  • Publicity which places a person in a false light in the public eye
  • Appropriation of ones name or likeness

A Noise Society

"The collective expectation of privacy will increaase, but the individual expectation of privacy will decrease"

Not enough resources to analyze all members of society, but enough data to analyze specific individuals

To stay unobserved, it might be better to not use special encryption techniques, but rather just blend in and rely on large numbers.

Why pair privacy with security?

Privacy Laws in the United States

There is no one law regulating data collection. Insteaad, privacy is regulated with respect to specific kinds of data (e.g healthcare) or specific popultions (e.g children)

In the U.S, privacy laws often vary state to state

The data collected by the vast majority of products people use isn't regulated.

Increased Protections

Three states have passed data protection laws

Further protections include that:

  1. a company must tell you when they are selling your data
  2. you can choose whether they can sell your data or not
  3. you have the right to access, delete, correct, or move your data

California law is the strongest. It gives user right to sue companies after a data breach.

Virgina law is the weakest

Basic Protections

Data Collection and Sharing Rights

  • You should be able to see what data a company haas collected on you, request deletion of that data, migration to another service. Choice in wheather a company can sell your data

Opt in Vs. Opt out

  • A company should have to ask first

Data Minimization

  • A company should only collect the bare minimum it needs to provide the service

NonDiscrimination

  • A company should no be able to discriminate against people who exerciese their privacy rights (e.g wealth shouldn't effect protection)

Key Takeaways

The need for privacy is longheld. The notion of a right to privacy is newwer.
