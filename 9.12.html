Human Centered Design

Human Centered Design

The Design of Everyday Things by Don Norman

  • "Puts human needs, capabilities, and behavior first, then designs to accommodate those needs, capabilities, and ways of behaving."
  • "Ensure that the designs match the needs and capabilities of the people for whom they are intended."

Centers people and their needs, motivations, emotions, behaviour, andperspective in the development of a design. Involving people in design does not necessarily mean they are centered.

  • Human Centered Design sees humans as people

Inclusive Design

Good for people with disabilities, but often times good for the general public as well!

  • ex. Yellow strip on railway track

Exclusive Design

Designed to impede an action

  • ex. Benches making hard for homeless to sleep

Hostile Archetecture

Rationalistic vs. Humanistic

The Rationalistic perspective is primarily focused on technological innovation, efficiency

  • How can we improve our models, predictions, products?

The Humanistic perspective is primarily focused on improving human conditions

  • How can we leverage technology as a tool to make lives (of humans) or better
  • Sensitive to context, likely better at identifying unintended consequences

Drawbacks to Humanistic Approach

  • Rationalist is very foward facing (future ideal world), Humanist is very here and now. Generally this limits future innovation as a humanist

Schools of Human Centered Design Though

Human Centered Systems

  • Technology is interdependent on political, ideological, cultural assumptions
    • ex. Children not having to say please and thank you to Alexa
  • Technology is influenced by these things, but also influences these things

Social Design

  • The intent and role of the designer is a function of societal forces (ex. socioeconomics, cultural values)

Participatory Design & Inclusive Design

  • Participatory: Embed the target audience of the technology in the design process
  • Inclusive: Emphasizes the importance of designing for a wide range of human diversity
    • Computer Science's Scale is massive. Ex. Youtube's edge cases are millions of people.
