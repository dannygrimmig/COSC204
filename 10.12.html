The Old vs. New Digital Divide

The New Digital Divide

The priviledged are processed more by people, the masses by machines.

What are the features of machine vs. human processing?

  • Machines:
    • Cheap
    • Bulk
    • Unable to understand context
    • A priori resoning
    • Neutrality in Process
    • Design/Data Bias
  • Humans:
    • Expensive
    • Slower
    • Context/Specificity
    • Generative Resoning
    • Emotional/Cultural/Societal Bias

Machines process cheaply and in bulk, leave no room for ambiguity or context, inherit their biases from the data they are given.chosen of designers

Humans are expensive.slow, can consider context/make exceptions, are prone to bias individually (often inherited from culture generally)

Key Takeaways

A "digital divide" can refer to any discrepancy in the use or impact of technoloagy at a group level

The original "digital divide" refferred to the availability of technology being tied to wealth
