Copyright & Piracy II

Labor Theory of Property

Roots in philosophy of John Locke

Ties property ownership to the labor used to produce the thing

  • Unclear if this extends to non physical property

Here we see that fundamental to history theories of property is the assumption of scarcity

Utilitarian Theory of Property

Property is not derived from the labor used to create the thing

  • Rather, property is a practical convention of the state used to maximize good.

The protection of copyright allows creators to publicize their work, benifitting all.

Personality Theory of Property

Argues that labor and utilitarian theories require criteria external to the individual to determine property rights

In contrast, personality theory ties property rights directtly to the persona of the creator

  • Arguing that the created thing is an extension of the creator and through that relationship deserves protection

DMCA

Digital Millenium Copyright Act: 1998

  1. protections for online service providers if users engage in copyright infringement
  2. ecourage copyright owners to give greater access to their works in digital formats by providing them with legal protections against unauthorized access
    • ex: hacking passwords/circumventing encryption
  3. making it unlawful to provide false copyright management information
    • ex: names of authors and copyright owners, titles of works

Critcs:

  • Do you really own anything you buy? ex: buying book on audible, can only read it on audible
  • Discourages free expression and scientific research
  • Interferes with fair use rights
  • Impedes competition and innovation, sometimes far outside of its intentional scope
  • Apple has used the DMCA to force iPhone to use iOS

Right to Repair

The general idea that when you buy a thing it belongs to you, so you should have the right to do what you like with it (including repair yourself

Other goals of the movement:

  • apple uses rare screwdrivers to repair iphones
  • iphone slow google searches increase as new phones are released

Planned Obsolescence: the notion that a product is intentionally designed to have a short lifespan, so new product must eventually be purchased

France:

  • It is a crime to intentionally shorten the lifespan of a product
  • 2018: France sues Apple for planned obolescence in iPhone
  • Apple had to pay $27 Million

Copyleft vs. Copyright

Copyright states that a created work is protected against unauthorized copying or derivation

  • Copyleft: copies or derivations of a created work are freely avaiable
    • Banting and Best: "Insulin does not belong to me, it belongs to the world"
    • Tim Berners Lee (HTTP): made it free for scientist to add and retrieve knowledge via the web
  • Copyright: copies...

Creative Commons

Rather than have IP totally free to use or totally restricted, CC offers tiers of restrictions/features

For Example:

  • credit must be given to the author
