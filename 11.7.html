Disinformation I

Why do people fall for fake news

BuzzFeed news found that "75% of American adults who were familiar with a fake news headline viewed the story as accurate"

Why do people fall for fake news?

  • Credibility
  • Confirmation Bias
  • Uncertainty of the World
  • Appeal to emotions

People who "cite Facebook as a major source of news are more likely to view fake news headlines as accurate"

Types of Information Disorder

Disinformation: deliiberate attempts to manipulate public opinion through the systematic use of false information. The intention to deceive is important, and that intention may cause harm in an of tself

Propaganda: intentional manipulation of public opinion, but not necessarily using false information. Instead the truth is selective and emotional appeals are made.

Unintentional Misinformation: where false information is spread unintentionally

Fake News: deliberate dissenmination of news stories that lack verified facts, sources, and evidence. Fabrication is an important element. Often done for financial and or ideological gain
