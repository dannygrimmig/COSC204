9.21 | The Quantified Self

What is the Quantified Self?

You can use modern technology to gather information about ourselves.

This data can serve severall purposes:

  1. Analyzing the data might yield insights about ourselves
  2. Aggregating the data might yield insights about people or groups generally
  3. Keeping data for posterity might be useful (external memory, legal record)
  4. The data could be sold o private companies or exchanged for exonomic incentive

A central tenent is that more information is better

Personal Data Tracking is Not New

What is different now?

  • Eletronic Sensors: The shrinking of eletronic sensors
  • Smartphones: Embedded into smartphones (ubiquitous)
  • Social Media: Social Media normalized sharing (and oversharing)
  • Cloud Computing: Possible to offload large amounts of data, combuine with others for analysis

Key Takeaways

The Quantified Self referes to a movement that emphasizes the collection of massive amounts of personal data, for various aims

Critiques focus on unintended consequences of data collection, misaligned values, and a rejection of the notion that everything worthwhile is quantifiable
