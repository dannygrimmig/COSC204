9.2 | Discussion Stoppers

DS #1: People disagree on Solutions to Moral Issues

  • People disagree on fundamental issues in every academic discipline. That doesnâ€™t make the study or debate of those issues inconsequential.
  • That does not mean everything is up for debate
    • There are certain conditions that must be met for a claim or theory to qualify for debate among scientists (plausibility)
    • Similarly, certain rules or parameters must be met for a claim or theory to qualify as debatable in ethics
  • Discussions are often muddled by confusions about the type of disagreement in question
  • Are we having a disagreement about principles or a disagreement about facts?
  • Both sides of napster debate would argue that stealing is wrong
  • What they are actually disagreeing on is whether the act of copying proprietary information is stealing

DS #2: Who Am I to Judge?

  • Generally, people are comfortable describing different moral beliefs, but less comfortable making judgements about those beliefs.
  • Can you practice normative ethics without making judgements about the beliefs and actions of others?
  • Making judgements vs being judgemental

DS #3: Morality is a Private Matter

  • If this were true, what you believe to be moral would be moral.
  • This view could be described as moral subjectivism
  • Are there any problems with moral subjectivism?
    • depending on your morals... these could affect others

DS #4: Morality is something cultures decide

  • Moral Relativism is the belief that we should not (or cannot) make moral judgements about the behavior of people who live in cultures other than our own
  • Is this closer to descriptive ethics or normative ethics?
  • Moral relativsm is the normative extension of cultural relativism
  • Are there any problems with moral relativism

Moral Relativism isn't that Relative

  • Some research suggest evolutionary roots of moral decision-making that are universal, common to all human cultures
  • One theory is Haidt's (2001) Moral Foundations Thory that posits at least five moral Foundations

Moral Absolutism vs Moral Objectivism

  • Moral Relatvism = there are no universaly correct answers to any moral questions
  • Moral Absolutism = there universally correct answers to moral questions
  • Moral Objectivism = there can be more than one acceptable answer to some moral quesitons AND there are certainly many incorrect answers to some moral quesitons
  • Whate miight we use to evaluate the acceptability of answers to moral questions?

Key Takeaways

  1. People disagree about morality, so how can we reach agreeement?
