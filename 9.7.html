Social Contract Theory

Thomas Hobbes described a "premoral" state of the world in which individuals were free to act in ways that satisfied their appetites and avoided their "aversion"

  • Any individual is in competition with all others
  • Cost of freedom is a miserable existence

Or rationality allowed us to realize benefits of banding together (social contract)

  • Hobbe's contract states: we are willing to surrender some "absolute freedoms to a sovereign, in exchange for system of rules to protect eachother
  • In this frame, morality is grounded in self interest

Is (or was) the internet, when first concieved, a Hobbesian premoral state?

  • Divergence of social rules between the real world comunity vs social media community
  • Lack of true consequences

Playfullness: Is there an evolutionary purpose for playfulness?

  • Some researches argue: presence of playfulness in intensely social creatures might point to some adaptive advantage
  • Playfulness may help us navigate tense social situations in a way that does not escalate to violence

Criticism of Social Contract Theory

Social Contract Theory is minimalist

  • We are only obligated to act morally when formal contract exist
  • Ignores questions of morality outside of a social contract

Morality is passive

  • focuses on doing no harm rather than doing good

Rights Based on Contract Theories

Based on the assumption that all humans have certain moral or natural rights

  • For Example, the inalienable rights enumerated in the U.S Declaration of Independence

Rights protected by the state are codified as legal rights

  • Negative Rights: one has the right not to be interfered with in carrying out priviledges associated. (Example: no one is allowed to interfere with right to vote)
  • Positive Rights: entiled to under law. (Example: Right to an education)

Character Based Ethical Theories

Virtue Ethics focuses on the development of good character traits and habits

  • Ethics as a practical science (a practice/skill)

Moral persons are predisposed (through the development of good habits) toward moral actions

  • Recognizes that we do not deliberate over each moral question we're faced with on a daily basis

Criticism of Virtue Ethics

Fails to resolve conflicts between competing virtues

May be less effective in modern, pluralistic society where there are disagreements about the relative importance of different virtues

In conflict with the individualism present in many Western countries
